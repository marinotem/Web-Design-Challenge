Summary: Latitude vs. X

The purpose of this project was to analyze different weather changes in relation to your distance from the equator. This was accomplished using the OpenWeather API, which pulled data from hundreds of cities for our analysis.


One we wrangled the data, Matplotlib was used to analyze locations, their weather and their relation to the equator. Some of the aspects we looked into were latitude in relation to: max cloudiness, max humidity, max temperature and max windiness. In the Plots part of this site, you can find more in-depth graphs, correlations and analyses of these weather conditions in relation to latitude.


The strongest correlation we see here in the data on this site is between temperature and latitude; in short, it seems that as latitude increases, temperature will overwhelmingly decrease and this effect becomes more profound once you cross the equator. This could be due to the tilt of the Earth on its axis that would provide this bias.
